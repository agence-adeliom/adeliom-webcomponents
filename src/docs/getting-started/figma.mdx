# Adeliom WebComponents Figma file

This document will guide you through the usage of this comprehensive UI kit designed to accelerate and enhance your web design process. Our components are tailored for efficiency, adaptability, and ease of use, making your journey from concept to prototype smoother and more intuitive.

## Getting Started

To begin using the **Adeliom WebComponents** in your projects, first ensure you have access to the file:

1. Visit the [Adeliom WebComponents Figma File](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1311342749870424489/adeliom-webcomponents).
2. Click on **Duplicate** to add it to your Figma workspace.

## Component Overview

Our UI kit includes a wide range of components, each meticulously crafted to meet the diverse needs of web design. Here's an overview of what you'll find:

- **Buttons**: A variety of buttons, including different states like hover, clicked, and disabled.
- **Input Forms**: Text fields, checkboxes, radio buttons, and toggles.
- **Navigation Bars**: Different styles of navigation elements for web applications.
- **Modals & Pop-ups**: Pre-designed modals for alerts, confirmations, and more.
- **Cards & Tiles**: Versatile card components for displaying content and information.
- **Typography**: A set of typographic elements for headings, paragraphs, and captions.

## How to Use

### Customizing Components

1. **Select a Component**: Browse through the component library and select the one you wish to use.
2. **Edit and Customize**: All components are editable. You can change colors, text, and shapes to fit your design needs using our predefined design tokens.

### Implementing in Your Project

1. **Drag & Drop**: Simply drag and drop components into your design frame.
2. **Combining Components**: Components can be combined to create complex layouts. Experiment with different combinations to achieve your desired look.

## Collaboration and Prototyping

- **Team Collaboration**: This UI kit is designed for team use. Share the file with your team members for a consistent design approach.
- **Prototyping**: Link components and frames to demonstrate user flows and interactions.

## Export your design tokens

To export your design tokens, in CSS for AWC, you can use our Figma plugin [adeliom-figma-devmode](https://www.figma.com/community/plugin/1309621381500130306/adeliom-webcomponents-for-figma-dev-mode) to export them.
